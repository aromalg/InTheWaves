4. Mass media

A. Mass media vocabulary

Radio, TV, newspapers. All of them belong to mass media. Do you know the meaning of the following words?

Television

Generalist Television: A TV channel that offers a wide variety of programs, including news, entertainment, sports, and documentaries, aiming to appeal to a broad audience. Example: RTVE.

Private Television: A TV channels or networks that are owned by private companies or individuals, rather than the government. These channels often generate revenue through advertisements and subscriptions. Examples: Antena 3, Telecinco

Specialized Television: A TV channel that focuses on specific types of content, such as sports, movies, or cooking shows, targeting a particular interest group. Example: HISTORIA

Platform Television: A service or app (like Netflix or YouTube) that allows users to access TV shows and movies on demand, usually via the internet, instead of traditional broadcast TV. Example: Atresmedia, Mediaset
 

Radio

Radio Station: A broadcast service that transmits radio programs, including music, news, and talk shows, through the airwaves for listeners to tune into via radios or online. Example: La Ser, Ondacero.

Radio Podcast: A digital audio program available on demand, often in the form of a series, which listeners can download or stream. It’s like a radio show but available whenever you want.

Newspaper

Tabloid: A type of newspaper or magazine that focuses on sensational stories, gossip, and celebrity news, often with bold headlines and pictures.

Broadsheet: A type of newspaper that focuses on serious news, politics, and in-depth analysis, typically with more text and less sensationalism compared to tabloids.
