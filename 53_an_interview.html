5.3. An interview

Sample text

OLIVIA RODRIGO: "MUSIC IS A WAY TO EXPRESS EMOTIONS"

Marta Marín. Seville, 25th March 2025.

Olivia Rodrigo playing the guitar for he photo shoot.


Many teenagers around the world love Olivia Rodrigo’s music. She became famous with her song Drivers License and now she is one of the most popular young singers. In this interview, she talks about her career, her inspiration, and her advice for young artists.

Interviewer: Hello, Olivia! Thank you for talking with us today.

Olivia Rodrigo: Hi! I’m happy to be here.

Interviewer: Many teenagers love your music. How did you start your career?

Olivia Rodrigo: I started singing when I was a child. I also acted in TV shows. Then, I wrote my first song and became a singer.

Interviewer: Your songs are very emotional. Where do you get your inspiration?

Olivia Rodrigo: I write about my life and my feelings. I think music is a way to express emotions.

Interviewer: What is your favorite song from your albums?

Olivia Rodrigo: It’s hard to choose! But I really love Drivers License because it changed my life.

Interviewer: What do you do when you are not working?

Olivia Rodrigo: I like reading, watching movies, and spending time with my friends.

Interviewer: What advice do you have for young people who want to be singers?

Olivia Rodrigo: Work hard and believe in yourself. Write your own songs and practice a lot!

Interviewer: Thank you, Olivia! Good luck with your music!

Olivia Rodrigo: Thank you so much!

Olivia Rodrigo continues to inspire young people with her music and her message. During our conversation, she smiled when talking about her fans and showed how much she appreciates them. With her talent and passion, she has become one of the most successful artists of her generation. Fans can’t wait to hear more from her in the future!

Structure

An interview is a journalistic genre in which a dialogue with a public figure is presented. It is a planned conversation, meaning the interviewer prepares questions in advance to learn about the interviewee’s opinions, thoughts, knowledge, and experiences.


An interview usually has three main parts:

Headline:

  • It should be short and interesting.
  • It can include a key phrase from the interviewee to attract attention.

Introduction:

  • Provides background information about the interviewee.
  • Explains why the person is important or relevant.
  • Mentions the main topic of the interview.

Dialogue (Questions and Answers):

  • The interviewer asks questions, and the interviewee responds.
  • The questions should be clear and related to the topic.
  • The conversation should be natural and informative.

Conclusion (Optional)

  • A short closing statement or a final message from the interviewee.
  • It can include a thank-you note from the interviewer.

Dr. Jane Smith is a well-known scientist and educator who has dedicated her career to inspiring young people to explore science. She works on projects that help teenagers understand space, technology, and the environment. In this interview, she shares her passion for science, her work with students, and her advice for young future scientists. Interviewer: Hello, Dr. Smith! Thank you for joining us today.

Dr. Jane Smith: Hello! I’m happy to be here and talk about science with you.

Interviewer: Many teenagers are curious about science. How did you become a scientist?

Dr. Jane Smith: I have always loved asking questions about the world. When I was a child, I enjoyed looking at the stars and reading about space. Later, I studied physics and became an astrophysicist.

Interviewer: That sounds amazing! You also work with young students. Why is that important to you?

Dr. Jane Smith: Teenagers have great ideas and creativity. I believe the next big scientific discoveries will come from young minds. That’s why I love working with students and encouraging them to explore science.

Interviewer: What is the most exciting project you have worked on?

Dr. Jane Smith: One of my favorite projects was helping high school students build a small satellite. We launched it into space, and they could see their own work orbiting Earth!

Interviewer: Wow! That must have been incredible. What advice do you have for teenagers who love science?

Dr. Jane Smith: Be curious and never stop asking questions. Science is not about knowing everything—it’s about discovering new things. Also, don’t be afraid to make mistakes. Every mistake is a step toward learning.

Interviewer: Before we finish, do you have a message for young people who want to be scientists?

Dr. Jane Smith: Yes! Science is for everyone, no matter who you are. If you are passionate about it, follow your dreams and find ways to learn more. The world needs young scientists like you!

Interviewer: Thank you, Dr. Smith! It was inspiring to talk to you.

Dr. Jane Smith: Thank you! Keep exploring and discovering!

C. Comprehension activity 2

Say if the following sentences are true or false:

Question 1

a) Dr. Jane Smith became a scientist because she wanted to teach students.

Feedback

False

False – Dr. Smith became a scientist because of her curiosity about the world, especially space.

Question 2

b) Dr. Smith believes that teenagers’ creativity will lead to the next big scientific discoveries.

Feedback

True

True – She believes the next big discoveries will come from young minds.

Question 3

c) One of Dr. Smith’s favorite projects was building a large satellite with university students.

Feedback

False

False – She worked on a project where high school students built and launched a small satellite, not a large one with university students.

Question 4

d) Dr. Smith advises young people to always avoid making mistakes because they slow down progress.

Feedback

False

False – Dr. Smith encourages young people not to be afraid of making mistakes, as they are part of the learning process.

Question 5

e) Dr. Jane Smith thinks science is only for students who are already experts in the field.

Feedback

False

False – Dr. Smith emphasizes that science is for everyone, regardless of their expertise level.
