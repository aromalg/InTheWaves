5.1. News article

Model text

NEW SKATE PARK OPENS IN SEVILLE

By Laura Gómez

Seville, March 2, 2025

A young boy skating in the new park.

The mayor of Seville opened a new skate park in the city yesterday. With this action, he keeps his promise to young citizens, who claimed for a park since 2019.

The skate park is in María Luisa Park and was officially opened yesterday. Many teenagers attended the event and tried the facilities. The park has ramps, rails, and a special area for beginners. The city built the park to offer a safe place for skateboarding.  Carlos, a 14-year-old skater, said, "Now we have a place to practice without problems." The local government announced that competitions and workshops for beginners will be organized soon.

News article structure

News articles have the following parts:

  • Headline: The title of the news story. It tells you what the article is about in a few words.
  • Author, time and date.
  • Image and caption: photograph and a description of what you can see in the image.
  • Lead: The first part of the article. It gives you the most important information and answers the basic questions 5W (who, what, when, where, why). Sometimes you can add "how".
  • Body: The rest of the article. It explains the details, background, and gives more information about the story.

Useful language

Introducing the story:

"A new event took place in..."
"Thousands of people attended..."
"The event was held to..."


Explaining details:

"The main reason for the event was..."
"The project aims to..."
"This was the first time that..."

Giving quotes:

"According to..."
"As [name], a [job/role], said..."
"[Name] explained that..."


Describing the scene:

"The streets were filled with..."
"The crowd cheered as..."
"The weather was perfect for..."


Concluding the article:

"In conclusion, this event has shown that..."
"Looking ahead, people are excited about..."
"The local community is now hoping that..."

General tips

  • Be clear and concise: Keep sentences short and easy to understand.
  • Use facts, not opinions: A news article should focus on facts, not personal opinions.
  • Use quotes to make it real: Include quotes from people involved to make the article more engaging.
  • Stay objective: Don’t show personal feelings, focus on the facts and the information that readers need to know.
